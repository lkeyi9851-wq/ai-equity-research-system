# Probability Rules

```text
High:
Evidence is direct, current, already visible in actual numbers, and valuation inputs reconcile.

Medium-high:
Evidence is good but depends on forecast execution or non-live valuation data.

Medium:
Plausible thesis, but market expectation gap or magnitude is not fully proven.

Low:
Speculative, narrative-driven, or missing financial transmission.
```

Hard rule:

```text
If valuation inputs conflict, thesis probability may still be high, but rating confidence cannot be high.
```

Use rule:
- Probability scores thesis truth.
- Rating confidence scores whether the thesis can support an investable stock call at current valuation.
