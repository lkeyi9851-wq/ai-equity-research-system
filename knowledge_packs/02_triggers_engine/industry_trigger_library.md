# Industry Trigger Library

Use as starting points. Always adapt to company data.

Machinery:
- Revenue growth below high-single-digit level.
- Gross margin below latest actual level.
- Overseas margin compresses toward domestic margin.
- Receivables or inventory grows materially faster than revenue.
- Dealer financing risk rises.

Banks:
- Net interest margin falls faster than loan growth can offset.
- Non-performing loan ratio rises while coverage falls.
- Capital ratio approaches regulatory or management floor.

Semiconductors:
- Utilization falls below break-even range.
- ASP or gross margin declines despite volume growth.
- Customer inventory correction extends beyond one quarter.

Consumer:
- Same-store sales growth turns negative.
- Gross margin falls despite price increases.
- Inventory days rise faster than sales recovery.

Software:
- Net retention falls below expansion thesis level.
- CAC payback lengthens while growth slows.
- Free cash flow margin fails to scale with revenue.
